Unregistered securities — pros & cons at a glance
🏢 For the Company (Issuer)
✅ Pros ❌ Cons
Lower costs — no SEC registration fees or heavy legal bills Capital limits — strict caps on how much total money can be raised
Faster funding — skips lengthy regulatory review, raises money quickly Smaller investor pool — can mostly only accept wealthy, accredited investors
Privacy — no public filing of financials or business strategy Strict compliance — must still follow complex state laws and SEC exemption rules
🧑‍💼 For You (The Investor)
✅ Pros ❌ Cons
High return potential — early access to private deals not available on public markets Locked capital — strict resale rules mean you cannot easily get your money out
Discount entry — securities often priced lower due to illiquidity High risk — private early-stage deals fail often; total loss is possible
— Limited info — fewer financial updates make it hard to track performance
