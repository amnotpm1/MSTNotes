What is the Elite Black Label Program?

The Elite Black Label Program – Signature Series I is a private, unregistered investment offered by Manhattan ST Notes, LLC. It is structured as a Secured Note — meaning you lend money to the company and they promise to pay you back with returns over 360 days. Think of it like a private bond with quarterly income payments.

Your money is deployed into litigation finance — funding large commercial lawsuits, bankruptcy proceedings, and arbitration cases. The fund aims to profit from the outcomes of those legal battles. After your 360-day term ends, you may also participate in any additional profits the fund generates through the post-maturity bonus program (called ARC).

Important: This is a high-risk, illiquid investment. It is not listed on any exchange, not regulated by the SEC, and once you invest you cannot exit early. Always seek independent financial and legal advice before committing funds.

Return Architecture

Four layers of potential return, each with a different risk profile

📅

4× Rolling 90-Day Income Windows

Income payments at Days 90, 180, 270, and 360 — each calculated as a percentage of your principal. Rates rise each quarter: 7% → 10% → 13.5% → 16%.

Promised · Not guaranteed
🚪

Structured Liquidity Optionality

At each 90-day window you can elect to exit early and receive 100% of your principal back. If you don't act in time, your capital automatically rolls over for another 90 days.

⚠️ Miss deadline = auto-rollover
🛡️

100% Principal Certainty at Maturity

At Day 360, the full amount you invested is claimed to be returned to you under the Multi-Tier Principal Protection (MTPP) framework. There is no independent verification of this claim.

🚫 No third-party verification
🌟

Up to 1.5× Post-Maturity Participation

After Day 360, if the litigation cases the fund invested in generate profits, you may receive up to 150% of your original investment as a bonus. If no profits — you receive nothing extra.

Expect $0 · Treat as a bonus

Activation Starts on Day 361 — the day after your principal is returned at maturity
ARC Window Day 361 – 720 (a further 360 calendar days after maturity)
Determination Dates Bonus amounts assessed 4 times a year: April 30 · July 31 · October 31 · January 31
Subscribed Principal (A) The reference amount used in all calculations — your original investment
Participation Allocation Limit (PAL) Maximum paid out per quarter = 0.5 × A (e.g. $50,000 on a $100,000 investment)
Aggregate Cap (AC) Maximum total ever paid across all ARC distributions = 1.5 × A (e.g. $150,000 on $100,000)
Realised Upstream Proceeds (RUP) The actual litigation profits allocated to your note — assessed at each determination date
ARC Distribution Each payment = the lowest of: RUP, PAL, and remaining capacity. Protects against overpayment.
No Proceeds Scenario 🚫 If RUP = 0 → you receive $0. If the lawsuits don't win or generate proceeds, no bonus is paid — for that quarter or potentially ever.
Cap Control Once total ARC paid reaches 1.5 × A, no further distributions are made — the program ends automatically
Termination ARC ends automatically when total distributions equal the aggregate cap (1.5 × A)
